The GrepoData userscript is required to use the in-game City Indexer features.


Step 1: Install Tampermonkey

Tampermonkey is the world's most popular userscript manager.

Restart your browser if you just installed Tampermonkey.

Step 2: Install the userscript

Click the install button to the right and add the script to Tampermonkey (opens in new tab).
Install userscript

Step 3: Link the script to your account

  • Open or refresh your game window after installing the script.
  • The first time you try to index a report or view town intel, a popup will be displayed with an activation link.
  • Click on the activation link in the popup. This will open a new browser window at grepodata.com.
  • Sign in to your GrepoData account after clicking on the link. This will link the script to your personal account.

Done! You can now start collecting enemy intelligence.

Note: You do not have to update the GrepoData userscript. Your browser will automatically use the latest version.

‚≠ê Userscript features


Indexing Reports

Use the 'Index +' button as shown on the right to upload the report to your GrepoData account.
The index button will be added to reports in your inbox and on reports posted on your alliance forum.
When you join a GrepoData team, your indexed reports will be shared with your teammates.

Sharing Commands

Use the 'Share with team' button on your command overiew to share the commands with your GrepoData team. Your teammates will be able to see your shared commands in a real-time combined command overview.

Browsing City Intelligence

Use the 'Intel' button in the town context menu to open the town intelligence window. In this window you will be able to see the collected intel for a specific town as shown on the right.

You will also be able to view an overview of player and alliance intel on grepodata.com

Forum Reactions

If you have the userscript installed, you can leave reactions to posts on the in-game alliance forum. Your alliance members can see your reaction and you can see theirs, but only if they also use the userscript and are part of the same GrepoData team. You can disable this feature in the settings.
